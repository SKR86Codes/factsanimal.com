DEER
Deer are graceful, hoofed mammals found in various habitats across the world. They are herbivores, feeding on grasses, leaves, and fruits. Males often have antlers, which they shed and regrow annually. Deer are social animals, often found in groups, and they are important prey for many predators.

ELEPHANT
Elephants are the largest land mammals, known for their large ears, long trunks, and herbivorous diet. They live in Africa and Asia, with African elephants being larger. They have complex social structures led by matriarchs and face threats from habitat loss and poaching.

CHEETAH
Cheetahs are the fastest land animals, reaching speeds of 60-70 mph (97-113 km/h). They inhabit grasslands and savannas in sub-Saharan Africa and parts of Iran. Their diet mainly consists of small to medium-sized ungulates, like gazelles. Physically, cheetahs have a sleek, slender build with long legs and distinctive black tear marks on their faces.
